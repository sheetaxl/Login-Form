BRENDAN EICH

Brendan Eich is best known for creating JavaScript, one of the most widely used programming languages in the world. He developed JavaScript in just 10 days in May 1995 while working at Netscape Communications Corporation. The language was originally designed to make web pages more dynamic and interactive and has since become a foundational technology of the World Wide Web, alongside HTML and CSS. Co-founder of Mozilla: Eich co-founded the Mozilla project, which aims to promote an open and accessible internet. Mozilla is best known for developing the Firefox web browser. CEO of Mozilla Corporation: Eich briefly served as the CEO of Mozilla Corporation in 2014. His tenure was short-lived due to controversy over his political donations, leading to his resignation. Co-founder and CEO of Brave Software: After leaving Mozilla, Eich co-founded Brave Software, which developed the Brave web browser. Brave is known for its focus on privacy and ad-blocking features. It also introduced an innovative approach to digital advertising using blockchain technology and its own cryptocurrency, the Basic Attention Token (BAT).
